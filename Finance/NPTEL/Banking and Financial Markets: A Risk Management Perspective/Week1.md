### 1. Risk‚ÄìReturn Relationship

* Fundamental principle: **‚ÄúHigher risk ‚Üí higher expected return; Lower risk ‚Üí lower return.‚Äù**
* **Risk-free assets** (e.g., government securities, treasury bonds):

  * Very low risk
  * Lowest returns
* **High-risk assets** (e.g., high-yield/junk bonds):

  * High default risk
  * Higher expected returns
  * Risk of issuer insolvency and non-payment of interest/principal

---

### 2. Meaning of Risk

* Risk arises when a **decision maker has a view about the future**, but the outcome is uncertain.
* Example:

  * CFO believes **interest rates will fall** or **domestic currency will appreciate**
  * Decisions are made based on this view
  * If correct ‚Üí profits increase
  * If wrong ‚Üí firm may incur significant losses

---

### 3. Risk Management

* **Objective:** Protect the downside
* Focuses on **minimizing losses if outcomes are opposite to expectations**
* Example:

  * If CFO expects currency depreciation
  * He hedges against appreciation risk
  * Uses **derivative instruments** such as:

    * Currency futures
    * Currency options

---

## Types of Risks in Financial Markets

### 1. Credit Risk

* Risk of **borrower or bond issuer failing to meet contractual obligations**
* Includes:

  * Non-payment of loan installments
  * Default on bond interest or principal

---

### 2. Off-Balance-Sheet Risk

* Arises from **contingent assets and liabilities not recorded on the balance sheet**
* Examples:

  * **Letter of Credit**: Bank bears risk if importer defaults
  * **Forward contracts**: Counterparty may fail to meet payment obligations

---

### 3. Foreign Exchange Risk

* Risk of **gain or loss due to exchange rate fluctuations**
* Applies to:

  * Foreign currency‚Äìdenominated assets and liabilities
  * Revenues or costs in foreign currency

---

### 4. Interest Rate Risk

* Risk due to **volatility in interest rates**
* Affects:

  * Capital value
  * Income
* Prominent when **asset‚Äìliability maturities are mismatched**
* Key element of **Asset‚ÄìLiability Management (ALM)**

---

### 5. Market Risk

* Includes:

  * Interest rate risk
  * Foreign exchange risk
* These risks are **exogenous** (outside firm‚Äôs control)
* Driven by **macroeconomic factors**
* Important for firms with **trading books**, dealing in:

  * Government securities
  * Corporate bonds
  * Foreign currencies

---

### 6. Operational Risk

* Risk of loss due to:

  * Failed internal processes
  * Human error
  * IT system failures
  * External events (terrorism, natural disasters, etc.)

---

### 7. Liquidity Risk

* Risk of **inability to meet short-term obligations**
* Example:

  * Sudden mass withdrawal by depositors
  * Forced asset liquidation at discounted prices

---

### 8. Solvency Risk

* Risk of **insufficient capital or net worth**
* Firm unable to absorb:

  * Sharp decline in asset values
  * Mark-to-market losses

## Overview of Liquidity and Solvency ‚Äì Part 1

---

### 1. Importance of Liquidity and Solvency

* Liquidity and solvency are **far more critical for banks and financial market entities** than for manufacturing or trading firms.
* Reason:

  * Financial asset companies deal with **financial claims (money, deposits, securities)** rather than physical assets.
  * Cash flow mismatches can arise suddenly and on a large scale.

---

### 2. Meaning of Liquidity

* A firm faces a **liquidity problem** when:

  * **Cash outflows > Cash inflows**
* Applies to both financial asset and real asset companies.

#### Cash Outflows (Financial Asset Companies)

* Disbursement of new loans
* Purchase of bonds and securities
* Interest paid to depositors
* Repayment of maturing deposits

#### Cash Inflows (Financial Asset Companies)

* Fresh customer deposits
* Interest income from loans
* Proceeds from sale of bonds
* Loan repayments from borrowers

---

### 3. Meaning of Solvency

* Solvency refers to the ability of a firm to:

  * **Absorb losses using its net worth**
  * Net worth = **Equity capital + Retained earnings**
* Helps withstand:

  * Sudden erosion in the value of assets

---

### 4. Liquidity vs Solvency

| Aspect       | Liquidity                                                | Solvency                   |
| ------------ | -------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------- |
| Nature       | Short-term                                               | Long-term                  |
| Cause        | Cash flow mismatch                                       | Capital inadequacy         |
| Frequency    | Common, often cyclical                                   | Less frequent, deep-rooted |
| Impact       | Temporary stress                                         | May lead to liquidation    |
| Relationship | Prolonged liquidity issues can lead to solvency problems |                            |

---

### 5. Focus of the Course

* Emphasis on **liquidity and solvency risks in financial asset companies**
* Such entities include:

  * Banks
  * Financial institutions
  * Market intermediaries

### 8. Key Lesson in Liquidity Management

A liquidity problem can be effectively managed if the bank:

* Holds **adequate cash reserves**, or
* Owns **highly liquid securities**, or
* Has **credibility and access to short-term borrowing**
